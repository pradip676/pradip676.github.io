🔐 What is Vulnerability Scanning?

Posted on May 9, 2025 by Pradip Sapkota

Vulnerability scanning is a critical part of modern cybersecurity — it helps identify weaknesses in systems before attackers can exploit them.

Tools like Nessus, OpenVAS, and my own Python-based tool VulnX automate the discovery of:

  • 🛠️ Outdated software versions
  • 🔓 Misconfigured services
  • 📂 Open ports & services
  • 🧨 Known vulnerabilities (CVEs)

Why It Matters

In industries like banking, healthcare, and e-commerce, vulnerabilities can lead to data breaches, financial loss, and legal consequences. Scanning gives security teams an edge — identifying holes before attackers do.

How It Works (Simplified)

  1. 🔎 Identify live hosts and services (via Nmap)
  2. 🧠 Match against known vulnerability databases (like CVE)
  3. 📝 Generate risk reports for patching or escalation

Want to try it yourself? Check out my open-source scanner VulnX — it's beginner-friendly and educational.
